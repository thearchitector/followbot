Sprint 1

For Sprint 1 our goal was to have a robot that had functional legs and walk in a straight line. We did succeed in building functional legs. Before we settled on one design we were deciding between a simple four bar linkage leg or l-shaped legs connected by a bar.

After working with both legs we settled on the l-shaped legs connected by a bar. In our first iteration we lazer cut the legs, but they did not have enough support to bear the weight of the robot. As a result, we switched to 3-D printed legs.

To get the legs to move we connected four servos to an Arduino Uno. The two servos attached towards the front of the robot were attached to the l-shaped legs connected by a bar. These two sets of legs would move in opposite directions, similar to how a four legged animal walks.

At the center of the robot there was another set of servos, one on each side. Each servo had an l-shaped leg that helps lift the robot, so it can propel itself forward. However there was a flaw in our leg design, so the legs in the center of the robot were colliding with the legs connected by a bar. Thus the robot was unable to walk. As a result we were not able to fully reach our sprint goal.

During this sprint we learned about all the components that we need to focus on for Sprint 2. Our goals for Sprint 2 are as follows:

  • Upgrade sensors from IR to cameras &/or LIDAR
  • Create a data pipeline between laptop and robot computer to accelerate path planning algorithm development
  • Redesign and fabricate new body of robot
  • Work on traction for legs to propel forward
  • Iterate on design of the legs so that there is a better range of motion, no unintended collision between parts, faster linear motion, and improved weight bearing
