Sprint 1

For Sprint 1 our goal was to have a robot that had functional legs and walk in a straight line. We did succeed in building functional legs.

Before we settled on this design (reference image above), we were deciding between a simple four bar linkage leg or l-shaped legs connected by a bar. After working with both legs we settled on the l-shaped legs connected by a bar. In our first iteration we lazer cut the legs, but they did not have enough support to bear the weight of the robot. As a result, we switched to 3-D printed legs.

To get the legs to move we connected four servos to an Arduino Uno. The two servos attached towards the front of the robot were attached to the l-shaped legs connected by a bar. These two sets of legs would move in opposite directions, similar to how a four legged animal walks.

At the center of the robot there was another set of servos, one on each side. Each servo had an l-shaped leg that helps lift the robot, so it can propel itself forward. However there was a flaw in our leg design, so the legs in the center of the robot were colliding with the legs connected by a bar. Thus the robot was unable to walk. As a result we were not able to fully reach our sprint goal.
